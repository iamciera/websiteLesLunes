ABOUT

At Les Lunes Wine we lease our own vineyards, work our own vines, built our own cellar and even poured our own drains. We’re bootstrapping like the pioneering winemakers of California. Our goal is to grow wines that pay respect to the great wines of California’s past, before money, ego and points got in the way.

We do very little in the cellar because we spend time in our vineyards. Proper pruning, precise suckering, well-timed treatments and thoughtful weed management lets us minimize the pesticide inputs in our vineyards. We do this to preserve the indigenous microbes on the grapes and in the soils, which leads to healthier and more complex wines that don’t need adjusting or stabilizing.

Wines

2014 chardonnay, Manton Valley

This Chardonnay is made from own-rooted vines planted at the Dobson vineyard in 1972 at the foot of the highly active Mt. Lassen Volcano. The slightly oxidative winemaking style gives the wine length and showcases the power and uniqueness of the decomposed volcanic soils found at the base of Mt. Lassen.

2014 pinot, Petaluma-Gap

Grown on our leased vineyard, this pinot was100% hand destemmed, fermented cool and aged in neutral barrels resulting in a wine that shucks the North-Coast standard. This wine lively, light, and completely drinkable.
